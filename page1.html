About Pitbulls

Pitbull dogs are friendly and sweet nature which is a direct contrast to their harsh and aggressive looks. There are 5 different styles of Bullies which the American Bully Kennel Club (ABKC) recognizes and registers. Each type has its physical traits, but the overall lifespan of 10 to 12 years is consistent across the breed.

Pitbull Type Height Distinct Characterictics
Pocket Males: under 17” at the withers, Females: under 14” at the withers A variation of the basic Standard Bully. The Pocket Bully has the same breed type, build and body type of the Standard Bully, but it is a bit shorter when it comes to height.
Classic Males: 17” – 20” at that withers, Females: 16” – 19” at the withers A variation of the basic Standard Bully. The Classic Bully has body frames that are light and narrow with less overall body mass. It still possesses the usual Bully traits despite having a traditional Pit Bull look.
Standard Males: 17” – 20” at the withers, Females: 16” – 19” at the withers Standard Bully is compact and medium or large. It has a blocky head and body that is muscular. It has a bulky build and look with a substantial bone structure.
Extreme This class has no appropriate height or weight Extreme Bully has more body mass and a body frame that tends to be heavier. The Extreme Bully's defining characteristics are a wider look and more winkling.
XL Males: over 20” – 23” at the withers, Females: over 19” – 22” at the withers A variation of the Standard American Bully. The difference between XL Bully and the Standard Bully is that the XL Bully is taller, more muscular, more massive and bulkier.
